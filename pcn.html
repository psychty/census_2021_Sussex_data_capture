Primary Care Network view in Sussex

This page shows much of the data on the other pages but enables a quick glance view for understanding local Census data for Primary Care Networks (PCN) footprints.

What is a PCN

PCNs are networks of general practices which attempt to integrate primary care with secondary and community services, and bridge a gap between general practice and emerging Integrated Care Systems (Fisher, Thorlby, & Alderwick, 2019). Each PCN is made up a group of a number of GP practices forming a network that covers approximately 30,000 to 50,000 patients.

The first map shows publicly available data on the residential neighbourhoods where patients registered to a particular PCN live according to their GP record.

We have excluded LSOAs where less than five residents are registered to the PCN. The GP practices linked to this PCN are also included on the map.
